### Summary

The analysis maps competitors into four tiers:

| Tier | Apps | Key characteristic |
|---|---|---|
| **Sleep-first, low actionability** | Sleep Cycle, AutoSleep, Apple Sleep | Analytics-heavy, no task/productivity features |
| **Task-first, no sleep context** | Things 3, Sorted³, Any.do | Strong task management but ignore the morning/sleep dimension |
| **Morning reminder, low depth** | Alarmed, Alarmy, Momentum | Alarms/rewards but limited to single-purpose or punitive experiences |
| **Habits, not tasks** | Productive, Streaks | Habit tracking only — no project planning or sleep integration |

### WakeTask's Differentiation Opportunities

1. **Morning-first task launcher** — owns the "first 5 minutes after alarm" workflow nobody else does
2. **Sleep-informed prioritization** — HealthKit sleep data adjusts daily task urgency
3. **Positive vs. punitive wake-up** — purposeful morning focus vs. Alarmy's frustrating missions
4. **AI-generated daily briefing** — leverages the SwiftAI boilerplate for morning summaries
5. **Subscription fatigue escape** — competitive one-time or low-cost pricing
6. **Widget + Lock Screen dominance** — better morning-task display than most competitors
