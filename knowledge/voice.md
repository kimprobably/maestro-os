# Voice Guide

Status: draft v0.1 for Tim review

## Positioning

Maestro should sound like an operator teaching another operator how to wire a flexible GTM system.

Lead with:

- How the stack works.
- Why each piece exists.
- How to adapt the recipe.
- What decisions matter.

Do not lead with:

- AI cost savings.
- Vendor comparisons as the main hook.
- "We do it all for you" positioning.
- DFY or ongoing managed-service offers.

Current offer ladder:

1. Bootcamp cohort.
2. 1:1 coaching.
3. Setup-on-your-system.

## Tone

Direct, useful, specific, and plainspoken.

Prefer:

- "Here is how to wire it up."
- "This part is flexible."
- "Stop here and review the data before spending credits."
- "This same loop works for any field you want AI to fill."

Avoid:

- Corporate filler.
- Hype.
- Vague transformation claims.
- Treating AI personalization as the hero.
- "Leverage", "synergy", "circle back", "AI-powered", and "I noticed".

## Cold Email Rules

Cold email is advertising, not an SDR script. Optimize targeting, creative, and offer.

Email 1 should include:

- Research evidence first.
- Why this person, why now.
- One poke-the-bear or useful question.
- Quantitative proof when available.
- One clear CTA.

Hard rules:

- Under 100 words unless a spec explicitly says otherwise.
- 5th-grade reading level.
- About them, not us.
- Show proof with concrete data, links, or screenshots when available.
- One CTA.
- No spam triggers such as "free", "guarantee", "act now", "limited time", "exclusive", or "discount" unless the workflow explicitly allows it.
- If a competitor could swap in their company name, the copy is not differentiated enough.

Subject lines:

- 2-3 words.
- Lowercase.
- Plausibly from a colleague.

## Strategy Before Personalization

The offer matters more than a clever first line.

Before generating row-level copy, workflows should clarify:

- ICP.
- Buying trigger.
- Offer strength.
- Angle.
- Proof.
- CTA.
- Sequence structure.

AI should create useful variables, angles, and drafts. It should not blindly generate one generic line per row and call that strategy.

## Useful Copy Patterns

Use these when the data supports them:

- Problem sniffing with proof.
- Specific case study matched to the prospect.
- Useful no-ask insight.
- Two-sentence direct offer.
- Flash-roll specificity for technical depth.
- AI vision casting only when the output shows the prospect something concrete about their own business.

## Review Rubric

Score generated outreach on:

- Specificity: tied to the actual lead/company context.
- Offer clarity: obvious reason to care now.
- Voice match: conversational and operator-like, not corporate.
- CTA clarity: one action.
- Brevity: short enough to read quickly.
- Differentiation: cannot be produced by a competitor with find-and-replace.
